# Solomon Audit Lab

A working repository for studying AI systems that infer aspects of a person’s internal world through dialogue.

## Purpose
This repo supports research for **Solomon**, a conflict resolution system focused on helping people navigate disagreement, interpretation, validation, and repair.

The core question behind this repo is:

How should an AI system engage with a person’s inner world — emotions, meanings, interpretations, motives, and self-understanding — while maintaining integrity?

## Focus areas
- Conflict resolution and mediation
- Coaching and reflective dialogue
- Therapy-adjacent conversational systems
- Validation, motivation, and ethical boundaries
- Safety, escalation, and trust

## What’s in this repo
- Audit templates
- Comparative notes on products and open-source projects
- Test scenarios
- Prompt experiments
- Interaction design principles
- Safety and integrity checklists

## Working principles
- Validate experience without automatically validating interpretation
- Avoid false certainty about another person’s motives
- Prefer transparency over simulated authority
- Treat high-emotion contexts as safety-sensitive
- Design for integrity, not just engagement

## Current status
Early research and framework-building.
